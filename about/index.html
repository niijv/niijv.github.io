About

... me

My name is Elias Zeitfogel and I am, among many other things, interested in computer graphics / vision, social network analysis, machine learning, data science, web development and learning new skills. In my free time I like to do obstacle races and go mountain biking.

I graduated from the University of Technology Graz where I studied Computer Science and Software Development and Business Management. Some of my work there has been published in the paper Evolution of Reddit: From the Front Page of the Internet to a Self-referential Community? at the World Wide Web Conference 2014 in Seoul, Korea.

I also do a lot of freelancing, some projects I worked on can be viewed here.

... this website

After finishing my studies I finally found time to dive into Flask, a Python microframework, and the initial version of this website is the result of that endeavour. The website was originally created with Flask (including various extensions) and some Bootstrap and was hosted on a free tier of OpenShift. It offers a basic CMS with some simple models for blogs, project references or professional milestones and an admin dashboard. You are free to fork it on GitHub or use the version I prepared for Heroku. But shortly after that another project by the creator of Flask piqued my interest. Enter Lektor. After contributing to the project myself and watching it get open-sourced and publicly released a few months later with great success I was determined to stick with it. And what better way to do that than using it for your own website.
